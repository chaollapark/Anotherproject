## How many Marketing/Communication jobs are currently available?

There are an estimated 50,000 communication, lobbying, or PR related jobs in London. Based on the report linked to lobbying in London on Wikipedia and based on the Transparency International estimate of only 5% of lobbyists being registered, it's safe to say there are at least 50,000.

Another positive number for corporate communications job hunters is how close Brussels and Paris are. Both Brussels and Paris have numerous new jobs in public relations and PR agencies, and most firms also operate in Brussels (and sometimes Paris). Much like the PA (public affairs) scene in London, Brussels also has around 50,000 lobbyists. Although the transparency register and other sources claim there are less, it's clear that if there are 500 consultancies, some 13,000 organizations registered and in-house corporate communications professionals with dual roles, the number has to be around 50,000 – otherwise it would mean some of the organizations that lobby don't have lobbyists (which would totally not make sense).

When searching for these roles use the following keywords: external communications, corporate communications, crisis communications, government affairs, public affairs, et al. Usually they are better than lobbyists. Note, however, that lobbyists is at the center of the Venn diagram - it's the word that sums all the other roles up.

## What are the salary ranges for Marketing/Communications jobs?

### The Market In Brussels

Surprisingly, most roles, at least entry and mid-level, are better paid in Brussels. I was absolutely shocked when I saw roles for communications specialists with a salary of 40k on a London job board.

As a rule of thumb, I'd say the salaries in Brussels range from:
1. 28k for an entry-level role,
2. 40k - 60k for a mid-level role,
3. 80k+ for all other roles depending on the class (for example, a media relations director in-house will always be paid more than the same role at an NGO, while an association communications director will earn something similar to a colleague at a consultancy).

### The Market in London

London is much more varied, and most roles change significantly. Mostly, I'd say this is due to less regulation and a smaller pool of competition. Brussels, after all, is the most international city in Europe, and so many young people go to Brussels in search of policy jobs.

### General Considerations

PR agency or account executive roles (intended as director roles) have more or less the same salary everywhere, but junior roles have more of a uniform distribution in Brussels.

For job seekers, I'd suggest searching for roles in both Brussels and London. Moving for a few years can only be a good decision – both for your career and in terms of salary.

## What kind of experience is required for the job roles?

Unfortunately, there is no one answer to this question, especially when it comes to jobs in communications. Some people come from social media management, some from digital communications, etc.

The one thing that all these roles have in common is the strategic aspect. But this doesn't matter – because if you're reading this blog, you're likely not a senior consultant or director-level person. Likely you're mid-level, or someone looking for a marketing communications role.

When it comes to Government Affairs (lobbying), there are 4 main routes: the political route, the company man - internship route, the expert route, or the lawyer route.

I'm guessing there are similar cursus honorum in marketing communications and social media manager roles.

In my case, I worked in politics and got a job in a Korean conglomerate. However, I have friends who worked as lawyers before becoming communications managers, and I also have friends who did completely different jobs. I guess the rule of thumb is to work in an adjacent field and try many things out before settling in one role and building expertise.

**TLDR:**

- Make a Google Sheet of all the public relations jobs you applied to.
- Add cold outreach to PR professionals and other PR industry account executive(s); meet a 200 quota.
- Be aware of "Dream Traps" - these are high quality, very competitive jobs.
- Note down the PR jobs you applied to.
- What can't be measured can't be improved. The same applies to jobs: while applying, note down the position name, company, description, and how many follow-ups you've done and when. This will help you understand if you are applying for the right role and if you are using the right type of CV.

For every 100 jobs you apply to, you should get at least 3 interviews; if you don't, you know something is wrong with your content strategy. You'll likely have to change plan and modify your CV.

Why is this so important? Because this is the only way you can verify if your communications attempts (sending resumes) is on the right track or not.

- **Write a hierarchy of public affairs jobs**
  
  You'll likely have a large choice of jobs in marketing, corporate communications, social media, etc. Given this is likely one of your first jobs, it doesn't really matter what you get, but you should still have a list of priorities. This will give all your applications a unitary goal - so every little thing you do moves towards one goal.

- **Stakeholder engagement while applying for PR agencies**
  
  Building relationships with key stakeholders—such as professionals in PR agencies—can significantly enhance your job search. Engaging with these individuals can offer insights into the industry, provide guidance, and even lead to potential job referrals.

## Conclusion in 5 Steps

1. Organize your job search in a Google Sheet, noting each position and follow-up.
2. Set a networking goal to reach out to 200 PR professionals.
3. Beware of overly competitive "Dream Traps" and keep a balanced approach.
4. Measure success by tracking interviews per 100 applications.
5. Define your public relations goals and apply accordingly to stay on track.

With a structured approach, your job search in public relations will be more focused and effective, helping you to achieve long-term career success.

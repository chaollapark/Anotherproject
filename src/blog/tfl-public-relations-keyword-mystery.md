While building this website we researched keywords and funny enough TFL Public Relations was the top traffic generator. This led us to research why "Transport for London" is the most popular place to work as a public affairs, or media relations specialists - Something quite unusual to write about on a job board.

Perhaps in the joking spirit of our team, or for fun we researched it, and here are some of our findings.

## THE TLDR:
1. A quick search for TFL Public Relations will lead you to a rather boing page about public transport and their different roles in stakeholder engagement (Government Relations, Media relations, Customer service),
2. A second search will show you that many positions in PR at organization that manages the London Underground are well paid and comfortable public jobs that also happen to let you work in a field similar to crisis communication and corporate social responsibility - so it's a bit like a job at a firm without the stress and with government employee benefits,
3. We tried to search other sources like job satisfaction, social media, and other PR industry professionals, but nothing really seemed satisfactory - so actually we don't know.

## We really can't tell why TFL Public Relations is a keyword

If you dear reader, know why TFL public relations is a public affairs top keyword please do let us know! This article doesn't have a definitive answer, but it's our best attempt to get at it - our TLDR gives you general insight.

### Our three main guesses why it's the keyword for external relations and even lobbying professionals:
1. Most likely it's a mistake. Why would government relations professionals be interested in working for the London Underground? Most likely we made a mistake in the keyword filtering, so most likely this small effort to understand the interest in tube strikes is a fun - but useless effort on our side,
2. The London Underground is a dream job for people in the PA industry, and somehow, unbeknownst to us, stakeholder engagement and travel updates actually have elements of crisis communication woven in (THIS IS VERY UNLIKELY),
3. Somehow the google algorithm is semantically connecting the TFL Public Relations keyword with words like DEI & Diversity and Inclusion, and other keyword pairs such as crisis communication and corporate social responsibility.

## In conclusion we're using this unknown to get more SEO

In writing this funny-gag article we actually used almost all the keywords in the TFL Public Relations keyword cluster, which is absolutely fantastic. We even found out some key questions that people ask about the TFL, for example:
- "Who are the organisations and elected representatives that the entity works with?" 
- "What is the way the entity wants to hear views, ideas and suggestions from the public?" 
- and lastly some stranger questions like "How often does the team run a survey and where can the latest survey be found?".

Was all this useless? Perhaps, but it helped us get higher SEO rankings.  
But if anyone can tell us what the reason is we'd love to know! We did reach out to Transport for London about this, but we haven't got an answer yet. Perhaps the TFL really does need more people in Customer service and media relations.

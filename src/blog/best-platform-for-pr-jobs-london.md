**Lobbyinglondon** will always be the best PR and GA job board. We have 11 PR Jobs boards in Lobbying hubs and we also have a second SaaS product for public relations and corporate communications - why is this important?

It means that we'll always have the in-house capabilities to improve our online "PR agency" and our job alerts will always be the best. But we'll also always be the best website for job posters. While our competitors have to sustain their revenue with the markets they are in, we can charge much less thanks to economies of scale and also thanks to the fact we are aiming to make a vertically integrated experience.

## The PR Jobs London Landscape

London, much like the other lobbying hubs, has an underdeveloped lobbying job board landscape. The two PR career websites cost £1000 to post new jobs - this is above the global average for developed nations. Also, the London PR officer and media relations-related job boards don't have social media or main content-sharing tools - which limits the distribution of job alerts (and new jobs).

We aim to disrupt the market of PR agency jobs London by offering a better product for cheaper with a better user experience. Here are some of the main points:

1. Corporate communications manager(s) or account manager(s) in small teams don't have the time to send emails back and forth to get a job posting. On our site, you can set up job alerts and postings in 2 clicks.
2. Our price point will always be lower than competitors because we can spread out the costs between 11 job boards. This also means we will always have a slightly (or in this case greatly better digital marketing aspect) because we have a dedicated team to our main content.
3. Our service will always be neutral and our blog (when it gets better and less SEO-focused) will be closer to journalism. In fact, the job website is only a funnel in our media outreach and community relations. Through our job site, we aim to build trust and gather leads.

In short, our job website isn't meant to bring us profit; it's a brand management strategy.

## How Public Relations Jobs London Fits Into Our Grand Strategy

Since this website is for PR Jobs in London, it's probably good to share our PR strategies and general strategy to become the software provider for lobbying-related industries. Also, being transparent is the best way to promote our main content. It's also the best way to make every employee and their network part of the Zmantic vision. If everyone knows the goal, everyone can be a brand ambassador.

So, how does a PR website which caters to "PR agency jobs London" as a keyword fit into our strategy, and why did we start with the 11 websites?

### Why We Started With The 11 Job Websites (including PR Careers London)

Peter Thiel's no.1 suggestion is that Startups try to build a concentric ring of markets, starting from the smallest market and slowly building up to the biggest market. That is exactly our strategy - we want to build up from the lowest part of the Public Affairs vertical: job boards.

It's an easy market to enter, and it's still underdeveloped in almost all key lobbying markets, and it takes 2 weeks to build a product (the job board itself). Our thesis is this: Branding, Thought Leadership, and Networking can be done through a series of job boards. Also, given that no one has thought of covering all 11 markets, we can also carry a significant edge over existing players.

Our second thesis is that through our strategic planning, we can also overcome our direct software competitors (should any decide to enter the market). Because, while they compete against 40 entrenched players and soften the market to the thought of an AI tool to do parts of the work, we'll build revenue and a networking advantage. This will let us - hopefully - swoop into the market and buy out all our battle-tired competitors from a position of strength.

### How PR Careers London Might Build Leads for Our SaaS Product

As already mentioned, our second thesis is that low-hanging fruit that makes revenue beats a difficult product in an entrenched market - why?

In the monitoring market - which in Brussels alone is worth from 100 million to 1 billion - there are at least 10 entrenched competitors who have been in the industry since forever and have strong branding. Normally, I'd try to out-raise them and crush them, but in this case, there is no rush. They can't change business models and suddenly fire all their consultants. Moreover, if I were to compete with them, I'd need to fight a price war or a conventional marketing and sales war. In such a war, bodies (salespeople, etc.) give an advantage, so it'd be like some rag-tag army trying to fight a conventional war. My idea is to win through strategic planning: we'll build up the lower part of the vertical, raise a 10m or 20m round later on, and swoop in. In such a case, we'll have nullified their thought leadership through consistent content creation, and we'll also have cut away their sales advantage by creating a direct contact through my job boards.

In this case, not only do we win if no direct competition enters the market, but we win even if a direct competitor tries to fend off the incumbent players.
